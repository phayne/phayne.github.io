Galileo PPR Thermal Maps

Thermal emission maps of Jupiter's moons from Galileo PPR
Source:
Galileo PPR
Targets:
Europa, Ganymede, Callisto
Type:
Thermal emission maps
Wavelength:
17–110 μm
The Photopolarimeter-Radiometer (PPR) aboard Galileo measured thermal emission from Jupiter's Galilean moons, providing temperature maps that reveal surface thermophysical properties and geological variations. ## Scientific Background Surface temperatures on icy moons are controlled by solar heating, thermal inertia, and internal heat flow. PPR observations constrain the thermal inertia of surface materials, helping to distinguish between fine-grained frost and consolidated ice. On Europa, thermal anomalies may indicate regions of recent geological activity or enhanced heat flow. ## Dataset Contents This dataset includes processed PPR thermal observations with: - Calibrated brightness temperature maps - Derived thermal inertia estimates - Observation geometry and timing information *Detailed format documentation to be provided with data release.* ## Related Publications - Spencer, J.R., et al. (1999). Temperatures on Europa from Galileo PPR: Nighttime thermal anomalies. *Science*, 284, 1514–1516.
